### 1. `.git` directory scanned as a "skill" (self-note from: `.git`)

**Skill that triggered it:** `.git`

**Exact note text:**
> "No signals detected. Skill may be pure-text documentation. Consider adding a whitelist check to fast-path such skills."

**Root cause:** When `--all .` is used at the repo root, `base.iterdir()` in `main()` returns all subdirectories including `.git`. The scanner then processes `.git` as if it were a skill directory, finds no signals, and generates this self-note.

**Specific change recommended in `risk_scorer.py`:**
- **File:** `skill-security-auditor/references/risk_scorer.py`
- **Location:** `main()` function, line ~354
- **Current pattern:**
  ```python
  targets = [p for p in sorted(base.iterdir()) if p.is_dir()]
  ```
- **Proposed fix:** Add a blocklist for known non-skill directories:
  ```python
  SKIP_DIRS = {".git", ".github", "__pycache__", ".venv", "node_modules"}
  targets = [p for p in sorted(base.iterdir()) if p.is_dir() and p.name not in SKIP_DIRS]
  ```
